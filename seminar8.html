Last week: New possibilities for democratic innovations with digital technologies?

This week: New challenges for democratic innovations with digital technologies?

Wrap up of main take-aways from last week
  • Digital technologies can be used in many different ways in the design of democratic innovaitons.
  • There are both potentials and challenges related to this.
  • There are several challenges in studying digital technologies in relation to democratic innovations:
    1. How to isolate the effect of the "digital", since digital technologies often are combined with institutional changes.
    2. How do we define or characterise the "digital" element? Digital technologies can take many forms.

Student presentation

Deliberation in an Era of Communicative Plenty

Ercan, Selen A, Carolyn M Hendriks, and John S Dryzek. 2019. ‘Public Deliberation in an Era Of Communicative Plenty’. Policy & Politics 47(1): 19–36.

An Era of Communicative plenty

"The term 'communicative plenty' referes to the increased volume of information, communication and activities on offer in online and face-to-face forms in contemporary democracies."

According to the authors this characterise the time we live in.

Sequencing spaces for deliberative functions

Suggestion by the authors.

Expressive (justificataion) -> reflective (listening) -> decision-making function

Two tasks to improve deliberative system

  1. Identify/intentionally create more spaces for reflection.
  2. Identify and strengthen ways that spaces for reflection area linked to other spaces.
