Núñez, Lidia, Caroline Close, and Camille Bedock. 2016. ‘Changing Democracy? Why Inertia Is Winning Over Innovation’. Representation 52(4): 341–57.

(Reading for student presentation)

RQ: What determines support for implementing DI's among parliamentarians?

  • Survey MP's in 15 European countries.
  • Asks about four distinct democratic innovations:
    • Refefrendums (least popular)
    • Opportunities for citizens to set political agenda (most popular)
    • Involve interest groups
    • Mini publics
  • Find that support for all these are highly correlated and calculates one measure for general support for democratic innovations.

Finidings

RQ: What determines support for implementing DI's among parliamentarians?

  • Time in government
  • Ideology: Fringe parties more supportive (radical left, green, and radical right parties)
    • However, this distinction disappear when controlling for time in government, but among the main stream parties social democrats are still more supportive than other mainstream parties (liberal, christian democrats, conservatives)
  • But overall, MP's are more supportive than opposed to democratic innovations.

Critiques of democratic innovations

Types of critiques of democratic innovations

  • "Anti-democratic" arguments.
  • Democratic innovations are unnecessary and potentially even undermine existing representative democratic institutions.
  • (Certain) Democratic innovations do not go far enough to engage and empower citizens.

Pateman, Carole. 2012. ‘Participatory Democracy Revisited’. Perspectives on Politics 10(01): 7–19.

Who is Carole Pateman?

  • Most prominent theoretical proponenet of participatory democracy.
  • Wrote a lot about this in the 1970's (but she is still active!)
  • Citizen participation central to democracy, and as a means to counter existing power structures.
  • Recap on participatory democracy:
    • Educative/developmental aspect of participation
    • Democratisation of all aspects of life (both socially and politically)
    • Creation of a participatory society.
    • Structural change to transform "undemocratic authority structures".

Group work

Remember these theories of democracy:

  • Minimal/Schumpeterian Democracy
    • Main mechanism: Competetive elections for leaders.
    • Reasoning: Ordinary people are not good decision makers, but elections provide an incentive for rulers to not become tyrannical.
    • Main propnents: Schumpeter (1942)
  • Participatory Democracy
    • Main mechanism: Direct citizen participation in decision-making at all levels.
    • Reasoning: Participation in politics is a good in itself, because it educates and empowers people.
    • Main proponents: Carole Pateman, (Jean-Jacques Rousseau, Sherry Arnstein)

Each group:

  1. Take perspective of one theory of democracy (Schumpeterian/participatory)
  2. Prepare arguments for or against mini-publics and participatory budgeting.
  3. Make short speech in favour or opposition to MP's and PB.
  4. Prepare counter arguments to the other group.
  5. Discussion between groups.
